Exercise 29

An engine produces wooden staff with a length of = 1.2 m and a standard deviation of = 2 cm. The length of the wooden staff follows a normal distribution.

Probability that the length of a randomly selected staff lies between 1175 mm and 1195 mm.

ex29_mean=1200
ex29_sd=20
lower <- pnorm(1175, ex29_mean, ex29_sd)
upper <- pnorm(1195, ex29_mean, ex29_sd)
prop <- upper - lower
ex29_pct_value <- round(prop*100,2) 
answer <- paste('The probability that the length of a randomly selected staff lies between 1175mm and 1195mm is',ex29_pct_value,'%') 
answer
## [1] "The probability that the length of a randomly selected staff lies between 1175mm and 1195mm is 29.56 %"

Exercise 32

A certain population has an IQ (intelligence quotient) of = 100 with a standard deviation of sigma = 15. The IQ follows a normal distribution.

What is the range of the IQ of 95% of the population?

ex32_mean <- 100
ex32_sd <- 15
lower <- (1 - 0.95) / 2
upper <- (1 - 0.95) / 2 + 0.95
pct95_range <- c(qnorm(lower, ex32_mean, ex32_sd), qnorm(upper, ex32_mean, ex32_sd))
paste("The 95% range of the population ranges from an IQ of",round(pct95_range[1],0),"to an IQ of", round(pct95_range[2],0))
## [1] "The 95% range of the population ranges from an IQ of 71 to an IQ of 129"
